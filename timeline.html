Key Developments


Below, we take a closer look at some of the most important advances and how they affected journalism.

  • Postal Service

    Written correspondence was the earliest method of communicating over long distances. Disadvantages included very long delays in message delivery and difficulty overcoming physical barriers.

  • Telegraph

    The telegraph allowed messages to be carried over long distances much faster than a horse and rider could carry them. However, it was time-consuming to send long messages.

  • Telephone and radio

    This marked a true revolution in communication; they enabled people to communicate over long distances instantaneously, which marked a monumental shift from generally proximity-based news to the distribution of information across geographical boundaries.

  • Email

    Electronic mail also spearheaded a dramatic shift in communication. With digital message exchange, the speed at which mail could be sent was no longer limited to the physical constraints of postal delivery.

  • WELL

    The WELL is one of the oldest virtual communities in operation today. The Well is a text and information based community in which researchers and journalists can communicate via Internet forums and conversational threads.

  • Blogger

    Blogger was one of the earliest blog-publishing tools, which popularized the format of discrete posts, displayed in reverse chronological order. It opened up information sharing to a variety of non-technical and non-professional users, and allowed interactivity by letting readers comment on posts.

  • Napster

    The emergence of Napster marked the development of peer-to-peer file sharing, where files are transferred directly between two users' private computers. This was a radically new way to download digital documents and files.

  • Wikipedia

    Wikipedia is a free Internet encyclopedia, significant not only for the impressive wealth of information that it provides on a variety of topics but also for its highly decentralized, collaborative editing process. This is very different from traditional publishing methods.

  • Facebook, Myspace, Friendster

    These sites ushered in a new era of high social media participation, which is very important because it keeps people connected with friends, family, and others with shared interests. It also makes sharing information extremely easy for the average user. It is easy to reach a large audience, and to publish and receive instant updates about important events.

  • Flickr

    This website facilitated mass online photo sharing.

  • YouTube

    YouTube popularized online video sharing, making it extremely fast and easy to disseminate video content to a wide audience.

  • Twitter

    Twitter limits users to tweets of 140 characters maximum, which makes users focus on short, direct communication. It also popularized the notion of "following" other users -- ranging from friends in daily life to major celebrities or news outlets -- which is very important because it essentially lets users curate their own personal news feed.
